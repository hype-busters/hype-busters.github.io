Best-Worst Scaling Tool

A methodology for measuring preferences and perceptions in scientific language.

What is Best-Worst Scaling?

Best-Worst Scaling (BWS) asks participants to identify the "best" and "worst" items from a set. This forces choices between items, providing more reliable data than traditional rating scales.

📊

Better Discrimination

Choosing extremes provides clearer differentiation between items than rating scales.

⚖️

Reduced Bias

Eliminates cultural and personal rating scale biases by using relative comparisons.

🎯

More Reliable

Produces consistent results with smaller sample sizes than traditional methods.

How It Works

Participants see small sets of items (3–5) and select the "best" and "worst" according to a criterion. This process is repeated across multiple sets. The frequency of best and worst selections is then converted into continuous scores that reflect preference or intensity.
